The rule says in conjunction the letters स्, and त्, with श्, चवर्ग will get into converted to श्, चवर्ग and the ष् and टवर्ग, they get ष्, and टवर्ग. Only conjuction is needed, and not the order. स्तोः श्चुना श्चु: 8-4-40

  

  

(“त्”, “थ्”, “द्”, “ध्”, “न्”) comes in contact with either the letter “श्” or a letter of the च-वर्ग: (“च्”, “छ्”, “ज्”, “झ्”, “ञ्”), then it is replaced respectively by “श्”, च-वर्ग: (“च्”, “छ्”, “ज्”, “झ्”, “ञ्”)।  

  

  

ष्टुना षुः ८।४।४१॥  

When the letter “स्” or a letter of the त-वर्ग: (“त्”, “थ्”, “द्”, “ध्”, “न्”) comes in contact with either the letter “श्” or a letter of the च-वर्ग: (“च्”, “छ्”, “ज्”, “झ्”, “ञ्”), then it is replaced respectively by “श्”, च-वर्ग: (“च्”, “छ्”, “ज्”, “झ्”, “ञ्”)।  

  

There are exceptions are there:

  

न पदान्ताट् टोरनाम् ८।४।४२\|\|  

  

श्वलिट् साये।मधुलिट् तरति\| इति,  

तोः षि ८।४।४३॥अग्निचित्षण्डे।  

शात् ८।४।४४॥प्रश्नः। विश्नः।  

  

Except these exceptions, any conjunction in any order, will result in शुत्व, ष्टुत्व for सकार and तवर्ग.
