Dear Group,

  

It is believed by many, including शं.बा.दीक्षित - भारतीय ज्योतिषशास्त्राचा इतिहास, that आर्यभट says in the following गीति that the stars in the sky are fixed and the Earth turns around itself, thus causing the apparent phenomenon of the stars moving from the east to the west.  

  

आर्यभट says in गोलपाद:

  

४.९.१ अनुलोमगतिर्नौस्थ: पश्यत्यचलं विलोमगं यद्वत्।

४.९.२ अचलानि भानि तदवत्समपश्चिमगानि लङ्कायाम्॥

(Just as a person sailing in a boat in one direction observes the (immovable) hill going in the opposite direction, so also immovable stars are seen from Lanka (equator) as travelling westwards.)

  

In a writing by the late Dr PK Gode of BORI (Studies in Indian Cultural History Vol II p. 49), he cites the following passage from वेदान्तकल्पतरुमल of Appayya Dikshita.

  

स्वर्गपृथिव्यो: परस्परमुपजीव्योपजीवकभावस्य तुल्यतया स्वर्गो न प्रतिष्ठा पृथिवी प्रतिष्ठेति व्यवस्थितोक्त्ययोगात्। किं तु निश्चलत्वमिह प्रतिष्ठाशब्दार्थ:। तत्तु ज्योतिश्चक्रान्तर्गतत्वेन भ्रमति स्वर्गे नास्ति अस्ति तु पृथिव्यां पृथिवी प्रतिष्ठेति श्रुत्यन्तरात्।
आर्यभटाद्यभिमतभूभ्रमणादिवादानां श्रुतिन्यायविरोधेन हेयत्वात्।

  

I get the general drift of what Ayyappa Dikshita says. He observes that Arabhata's statement about the Earth revolving (and the heavens being fixed) is not true as it is opposed to what the श्रुति says. However, I would like to have a word-by-word translation of this statement of Appayya Dikshita.

  

Arvind Kolhatkar.
