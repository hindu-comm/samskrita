Namaste.

  

This announcement is not directly related to Sanskrit, but one of its daughter languages - middle Kannada (used by Kumara Vyasa). There is a great deal of overlap between the two.

  

For the convenience of those interested in studying works of great beauty such as Kumara Vyasa's bhArata, we have made available stardict files constructed from the dictionary published with<http://kumaravyasabharatha.blogspot.com/2011/07/blog-post.html>.

  

The dictionary files are linked here:see[tars.MD](https://github.com/vvasuki/stardict-kannada/blob/master/en-head/tars/tars.MD "tars.MD")and[tars.MD](https://github.com/vvasuki/stardict-kannada/blob/master/kn-head/tars/tars.MD "tars.MD").

  

These dictionaries can be used your favorite stardict software (I use GoldenDict on Linux and Android). On Android, one way you can install the dictionaries is by downloading the XYZ.tar.gz files, and extracting them (using an app like ZArchiver) to the corresponding SDCARD/dictdata/XYZ folder (which can be created using a file manager app such as TotalCommander).

  

If you have access to other digitized Kannada dictionaries - you are welcome to contribute to the[stardict-kannada project](https://github.com/vvasuki/stardict-kannada)and help keep Kannada readership and scholarship vitally alive!
