Dear list

  

Are there any Sanskrit commentaries on Brahma Purāṇa available? I am particularly interested in expositions of the word *tridaivatyā* in

  

*gaṅgeyaṃ saritāṃ śreṣṭhā sarvakāmapradāyinī*

*tridaivatyā muniśreṣṭha tadutpattimataḥ śruṇu 71.3*

  

Meera Kosambi in *Intersections: Socio-cultural Trends in Maharashtra* (Orient Blackswan, 2000, ISBN 9788125018780, [p. 23](https://books.google.co.in/books?id=XU8dmAiaZSgC&pg=PA23)) translates this as ‘derived from \[all\] three gods’. However, the meaning of *derived from* does not appear to be present in the word *tridaivatyā* which has the *ṣyañ* suffix, most likely in *svārtha* (by the *vārttika* *brāhmaṇādiṣu cāturvarṇyādīnāmupasaṃkhyānam* on A 5.1.124).

  

The *Vācaspatyam* says this on *daivatya*:

  

*daivatya¦ tri0 devatā svārthe ṣyañ . devatāyām “ārṣaṁ chandaśca daivatyam” yājña0 “ṛgvedo devadaivatyaḥ (devadaivatyo) yajurvedastu madhyamaḥ” manuḥ .*

  

Commentaries on Manu 4.124 also explain *devadaivatya* as *devā devatā asya*.

  

So if the*ṣyañ*in *tridaivatyā*is taken in*svārtha*, the word should simply mean ‘having three gods/deities’ (*tisro/trayo devatā asyāḥ*), and not ‘derived from three deities/gods’.
