There is no need of deriving every noun deriving from root, 

  

"सर्वाणीति गार्ग्यो वैयाकरणानां चैके"  

  

according to गार्ग्य, and a gr0up of वैयाकरण-s. It is according to शाकटायन that all nouns could be derived from verbal roots

  

  

"तत्र नामान्याख्यातजानीति शाकटायनो नैरुक्तसमयश्च ।" (निरुक्त--1.12)  

  

as per Nirukta. Reasonable standard would be to derive where the roots could be traced to the noun directly to the meaning as per Panini's rules and a discussion is found in Nirukta on this point.

  

  

In the present case, नाक is regarded a compound word न+अक as per Panini's rule 6-3-75 without going further derivation of क. The splitting is as follows कम् = सुखम्, तद्विरुद्धम् अकम् = दुःखम्। नास्ति अकमत्र = नाकः। न विद्यते अकं यत्रेति बहुव्रीहिः।

  

If the word क is somehow derived from a root, it can be derived from कै शब्दे. or कच शब्दे.अas per Ashtadhyayi 3-2-101. But it has no realation with the meanings of the word :

  

मारुते वेधसि व्रध्ने पुंसि कः कं शिरोऽम्बुनोः ।।Amara  

  

को ब्रह्मात्मानिलाकाग्निचित्तधीयमकेकिषु ।

विषावाहतशब्देऽपि सितवर्णे वसौ द्युतौ ॥

कं शिरोऽम्बुसुखे क्लीबं तत्रैव च कमव्ययम् ।-

  

which is widely different from each other or the meanig of the root.
