कर्ष was a small unit of weight used in weighing gold and silver. There is no unanimity as to its actual weight, different sources giving different results in different times. 

  

'गणितसारसंग्रह' by महावीराचार्य (IX c.) has the following table for weights of gold (संज्ञाधिकार श्लोक ३९)

  

चतुर्भिर्गण्डकैर्गुञ्जा गुञ्जा: पञ्च पणोऽष्ट ते।

धरणं धरणे कर्ष: पलं कर्षचतुष्टयम्॥

४ गण्डक = १ गुंजा

५ गुंजा = १ पण

८ पण = १ धरण

२ धरण = १ कर्ष

४ कर्ष = १ पल

  

अमरकोश (V-VI c.) at २.९.८५-८६ gives the following:

५ गुञ्जा = १ माष,

१६ माष = १ कर्ष,

४ कर्ष = १ पल.

  

मनुस्मृति (८.१३३-१३८) has its own table of weights for precious metals. It says that the coin called 'कार्षापण' is of the weight of one copper कर्ष, which, in turn, has been defined as १० धरण.  

  

शब्दकल्पद्रुम has १ कर्ष = १६ माष = ८० रत्तिका. It also mentions that in आयुर्वेद १ कर्ष = २ तोला or १० माष (राजनिघण्टु). MW too gives similar other definitions.  

  

There was a punch-marked coin calledकार्षापण in the times of the Mauryan dynasty.  Several pictures of these may be seen [here](http://coinindia.com/galleries-maurya.html).  

  

Arvind Kolhatkar.
