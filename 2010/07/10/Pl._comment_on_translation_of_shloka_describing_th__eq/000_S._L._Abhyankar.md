SLA's comments –

1. Above translation, especially of sentences 4 and 5 turns out to be different from the one available at  

<http://www.bhagavadgitausa.com/sat_chakra_Nirupana.htm> (see below)

2. The construction pItambaraVOiva-ivaQa-ivarcanaalaMkRta seems to be grammatically incorrect.

a. In the phrase **pItambaraVO: ivaivaQaivarcanaaiBa:** the word **pItambaraVO:** is adjective of **ivaivaQaivarcanaaiBa:** But gender of **pItambaraVO:** is masculine or neuter, whereas gender of **ivaivaQaivarcanaaiBa:** is feminine.

b. pItambaraVOiva-ivaQa-ivarcanaalaMkRta is appearing to be a single conjuncted / compound word. As per above analysis, **pItambaraVO:** being adjective of **ivaivaQaivarcanaaiBa:** the word **ivaivaQaivarcanaa** should not have been separately compounded with AlaMkRta

c. To be gramatically correct, the phrase should either be pItambaraidiBa: ivaivaQaivarcanaaiBa: **A**laMkRta or pItambarI to be a separate adjective of saa laaiknaI and ivaivaQa-ivarcanaalaMkRta as another separate adjective.

d. All the grammar seems to have become confused because of Aa**VOO:**

e. The capital H in the phrase “in the lotus of His face” (in the given translation) suggests that the translation is referring to the face of God. However, by the above analysis, it rather seems to be the lotus-like face of one, who meditates on naaiBaपद्मma\\

Translation available at <http://www.bhagavadgitausa.com/sat_chakra_Nirupana.htm> -

“Here abides Lākinī, the benefactress of all. She is four-armed, of radiant body, is dark (in complexion), clothed in yellow raiment and decked with various ornaments, and exalted with the drinking of ambrosia. By meditating on this Navel Lutus the power to destroy and create (the world)is acquired. *Vāṇī* with all thewealth of knowledge ever abides in the lotus of His face.” . From *Ṣaṭ-cakra-nirūpaṇa* (21)
