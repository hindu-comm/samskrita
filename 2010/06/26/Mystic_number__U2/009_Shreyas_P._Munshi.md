In one commentary on Patanjali Yoga sUtras, I remember the commentator describing  
'mamasthAnas' as sensitive spotsin the body. ('hridaye samvit', 'nAbhichakre vyuhagnyanam' etc).  
Perhaps, the 'chakras' of trantra are the phychic nerve cenres which provide the sensitivity to the  
marmasthAnas. Since yoga technology consists in using the gross to move towards the subtle,  
the sAdhaka would, perhaps, benefit by concentrating initially on the marmasthAnas to be able to  
progressvely take the uplifing route of the chakras and then finallyreach the Cause and Source of the  
spiritual energy of the chakras. On basis of the scholarly views presented by the fraternity on marmas  
and chakras, the 108 beads of the mala thus seem to bethe grossest means forhelping a beginning  
sAdhaka in his journry on the spiritual path...an example of the teachng brilliance of our Rishis!

नमो नमः

AboutNumber 18 -

(1)Total chapters in श्रीमद्भगवद्गीता

(2)Number of पर्वाः in महाभारतम्

(3)Total army which fought in the war 18 अक्षौहिणी

(4)Number of days the war lasted

(5)Number of मन्त्राः inईशावास्योपनिषत् (excludingthe शान्ति-मन्त्रः)

(6)108, 1008, 10008 are all numbers divisible by 18. Such numbers along with श्री make a decoration suchas श्री१०८, which isseen in the list of decorations of शङ्कराचार्याःI guess, more the number of zeros between 1 and 8, more learned andrespectable a शङ्कराचार्यः is

(7)A जपमाला for count ofone hundred has 108 beads.

(8)In योगक्रियाः Numberof भस्रिकाः to bepractised with each nostril closed  
\>\>(9) It seems the arithmetic significance starts with 9, than 18. In themultiples of number 9, digits in every multiple of number of 9, right up to9x30 add up to 9, except (9x11). Yet even in 9x11 = 99 (9+9=18 --> 1+8 =9).Likewise in 9x21 = 189 (1+8+9 = 18 --> 1+8 = 9) Multiples of 1 in unit placewill have this extended summation.

Allthis yet does not clarify "why is 18 mystic ?"
