> > 
> > Vowels - the sound of the letter versus the sound of the vowel when > used in words - >
> 
> > 
> > A - (अ) when used in words could be read as any one of the devnagari > vowels  (अ , आ , ए, ऍ) >
> 
> > 
> > E - (इ) when used in words becomes like devnagari(ए) >
> 
> > 
> > I - (आय) when used in words becomes like devnagari (इ / ई) or like > the "name" of the vowel E. >
> 
> > 
> > O - (ओ) when used in words becomes like devnagari (अओ ऑ) and when > used twice becomes like an extended version of the vowel U. >
> 
> > 
> > U - (यू) when used in words can take the forms of any of the > following - "a" as in but, cut OR when "e" is used after the > consonant following "u" it become like its name. >
