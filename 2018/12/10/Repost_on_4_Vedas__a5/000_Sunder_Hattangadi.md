> Fortunately, several publications on the Vedas are available in the > market today. There are various translations, most of them competent. > We already distribute a few of the Hindi and English translations. But > for a long time, a need has been felt to have the complete Sayana > bhasya, the legendary Sanskrit commentary by Acarya Sayana, available > in modern Hindi. Also, while most publications offer the original text > of the Vedas, only one offered the original Vedic Sanskrit text along > with the appropriate svaras {accents}. And even there, the accents are > not accurately printed. >
> 
> >   
> >  style="line-height:1.2;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt"> >
> Nearly thirty years ago, a project was set in motion. To produce a set > of Vedas that have the original Vedic Sanskrit text with appropriate > accents, along with an exhaustive Hindi commentary, based on the > original bhasya {exposition} of Acarya Sayana. And to fill a > historical gap. Sayana’s exhaustive commentary was not available for > chapters 11 to 16 of the Atharvaveda. A fresh commentary had to be > written on these chapters. In addition, the excellent commentaries by > Uvatamahidhara, Acarya Panini, Skanda Svami, Pandit Shripad Damodar > Satavalekar and others had to be consulted and cited. Thus, a fresh > exhaustive, traditionally sound and yet contemporary commentary on the > Vedas had to be written. >
> 
> >   
> >  style="line-height:1.2;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt"> >
> Six leading Vedic scholars of India were involved in this project: >
> 
> >  style="line-height:1.2;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt"> >
> Acarya Dr Munshiram Sharma; Dr Janardan Gangadhar Ratate; Dr Sudhakar > Malaviya; Dr Kunvar Chandraprakash Singh; Dr Maheshchandra Sharma and > Pt Jatashankar Dikshit. >
> 
> >   
> >  style="line-height:1.2;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt"> >
> This text is the fruit of their labour and deliberations. It is an > excellent, low priced edition of the four Vedas, along with a complete > Hindi commentary based on the seminal bhasya by Acarya Sayana. >
