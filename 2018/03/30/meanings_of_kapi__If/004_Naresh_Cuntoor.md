Shabdaartha-kaustubha gives a few derivations for kapi -  

कम्पते इति कपिः (इन् इति उणादिप्रत्ययः)  

कम् (सुखं) पाति (रक्षति) इति कपिः (=विष्णुः) ; इन् प्रत्ययः  
  

And as shown in the document that Sri Sundar sent, we see -  

कम् (जलं) रश्मिभिः पिबति इति कपिः (=सूर्यः), इन् प्रत्ययःम्  
  

In other words, there are three different derivations for three different meanings of kapi - monkey, Vishnu and sun.  
  

The Unaadi suffix "in" gives the "pi."  

  

If the intention is to ignore all these derivations and jump to a hypothetical Proto Indo-European source, one is free to do so.
