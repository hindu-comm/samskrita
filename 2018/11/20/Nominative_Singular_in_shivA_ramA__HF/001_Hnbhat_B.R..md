It is lost due to the Panini's ruleहल्ङ्याब्भ्यो दीर्घात्‌ सुतिस्यपृक्तं हल्  
The English translation of the rule is "  
The सुँ, ति, or सि प्रत्यय, when present as a single letter, is removed when attached to - (1) a word ending in a हल् letter, or (2) A दीर्घ word ending in the ङीप् / ङीष् / ङीन् / चाप् / टाप् / डाप् प्रत्यय.

Other examples areराजा,गौरी,

If you can read Sanskrit, you can have more details of the rule here:

<http://www.ashtadhyayi.com/sutraani/6/1/68>
