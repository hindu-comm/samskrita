I thik you have not tried Siddhantakaumudi started from the beginning sanjnaprakarana, and panca sandhiprakarana and directly from Subanta prakarana. The rules are quoted then and there as the case occurs and you have to remember sutras mentioned earlier applicable to the context. If you cannot remember, any commentary like Balamanorama will help. All the words in respect of a-karanta etc. are explained there, but only you have to understand the necessary rules from the examples given therein. Ashtadhyayi does not offer subanta-s in that order and you have to learn words prakriya according to your teacher's advice or take the help of the commentary on SK if yourself can not learn the prakririya from SK itself.

  

Anyhow it is up to you. I have nothing to advice. I had learnt from SK only with the help of a commentary. Wish you best of luck in your endeavours.
