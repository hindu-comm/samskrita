Mr P,

  

Knowing the particular font, the Sanskrit could be edited directly.

  

Otherwise as a workaround you can do thusly:

  

1. Take the transliteration:

  

oṁ taccaṁ॒ yorāvṛ॑ṇīmahe \| gā॒tuṁ ya॒jñāya॑ \| gā॒tuṁ ya॒jñapa॑taye \| daivī॓ sva॒stira॑stu naḥ \| sva॒stirmānu॑ṣebhyaḥ \| ū॒rdhvaṁ ji॑gātu bheṣa॒jam \| śaṁ no॑ astu dvi॒pade॓ \| śaṁ catu॑ṣpade \|

oṁ śāṁtiḥ॒ śāṁtiḥ॒ śāṁtiḥ॑ \|\|

  

2. Delete the unwanted box characters (they represent two kindsofspecialcharacters that aid chanting (pronunciation), to get:

  

oṁ taccaṁ yorāvṛṇīmahe \| gātuṁ yajñāya \| gātuṁ yajñapataye \| daivī svastirastu naḥ \| svastirmānuṣebhyaḥ \| ūrdhvaṁ jigātu bheṣajam \| śaṁ no astu dvipade \| śaṁ catuṣpade \|

oṁ śāṁtiḥ śāṁtiḥ śāṁtiḥ \|\|

  

3. Then convert the cleaned transliteration back to Devanagari, using a font of your choice..

  

  

Hope this helps,
