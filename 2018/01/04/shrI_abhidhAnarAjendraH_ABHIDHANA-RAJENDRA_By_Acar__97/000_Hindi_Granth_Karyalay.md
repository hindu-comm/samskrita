Notes  
Unsurpassed as the most comprehensive and authentic encyclopedia on Jainism

Explains nearly 60,000 Prakrit terms, gives their Sanskrit equivalents

Provides over 400,000 explanatory citations in Sanskrit

  

The ‘Abhidhana Rajendra’ merits recognition as the 20th century’s finest encyclopedic work on Jainism. For generations of scholars, it remains the go-to encyclopedia on Jainism.

  

A result of the Herculean efforts of Acarya Rajendrasuri, it is a magnificent repository of citations sourced from a wide selection of Jain texts belonging to both, the Digambara as well as the Shvetambara traditions. It is a gigantic set of seven volumes, each running into thousands of pages. It covers a very substantial selection of Prakrit terms, each explained in Sanskrit using citations from various Jain texts and commentaries. It is perhaps the most outstanding Jain publication of the 20th century.

  

Salient features:

-   Original Ardhamagadhi Prakrit term given

-   Sanskrit equivalent provided

-   Information on the gender and etymology of the Prakrit term

-   Comprehensive description as per the Agamas and commentaries

-   Multiple citations provided with proper cross references

-   Arranged in alphabetical order

-   Jain technical terms exhaustively explained

-   97 Original Jain texts were referred to while creating this
    encyclopedia  
      

About the author  
Acarya Rajendra Suri (1827 - 1906) was an exceptional genius. He was a mendicant leader of the Shvetambara Murtipujaka Tristutika Gaccha. He played an important role in the restoration of Jain temples and advocated the printing of sacred Jain texts and their study by Jain ascetics. In addition to creating the magnificent Abhidhana Rajendra encyclopedia, he also wrote commentaries on the Agamas and composed several hymns.

  

Publication history

Acarya Rajendra Suri prepared this encyclopedia single-handedly, over a period of 14 years.

1.  First published in 1903

2.  Reprinted in 1984

3.  Freshly typeset and published in 2014
