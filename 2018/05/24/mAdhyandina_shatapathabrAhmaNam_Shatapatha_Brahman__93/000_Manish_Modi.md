The Vedic Corpus

RgVeda, YajurVeda, SamaVeda and AtharvaVeda form the apex of the Vedic Corpus

AyurVeda, DhanurVeda, GandharvaVeda and ArthaVeda are the UpaVedas, or minor Vedas   
Pratishakhyas were composed in order to facilitate a deeper understanding of the Vedas

Brahmana texts, Grihya Sutras, Shrauta Sutra, Aranyakas and Upanishads complete the corpus

  

The Brahmanas

Authentic exegetical works on the Vedic Samhitas

Expound on worship rituals and mantras relevant to worship rituals

Precisely define Vedic mantras

Standardise Vedic rituals in accordance with specific Vedic instructions

Explain Genesis as per the Vedic religion

  

Separate Brahmana texts exist for each Veda. There are thirteen Brahmana texts

RgVeda : Aitareya Brahmana, Kaushitaki Brahmana

Krishna YajurVeda : Taittariya Brahmana

Shukla YajurVeda : Shatapatha Brahmana

SamaVeda : Tandra Brahmana, Shadvimsha Brahmana, Arsheya Brahmana, Samavidhana Brahmana, Samhitopanishad Brahmana, Mantra Brahmana, Devatadhyaya Brahmana & Vamsha Brahmana

AtharvaVeda : Gopatha Brahmana

  

Shatapatha Brahmana {Brahmana of One Hundred Chapters}

Expounded by Rishi Yajnavalkya

Divided into 14 books, with 100 chapters and 7,624 verses

Includes the celebrated Brihat Aranyaka Upanishad, which is considered to be the loftiest peak of Vedic wisdom

Describes Vedic rituals and the history and beliefs associated with the Shukla YajurVeda

Explains in great detail the preparation of altars, ceremonial objects, ritual recitations, and the Soma libation, along with the symbolic attributes of every aspect of the ritual

Acarya Sayana wrote the ‘Vedarthasara’ Sanskrit commentary on this text

Acarya Harisvami also wrote a Sanskrit commentary on this text

Has two major recensions: Madhyandini Shakha and the Kanva Shakha

The current work is based on the Madhyandini Shakha recension
