The website/online journal also carries many other articles in Sanskrit (both originally in Sanskrit and translated into Sanskrit) -- search with Language set to Sanskrit on <https://www.prekshaa.in/archive>.

•Satirical Humour in Modern Sanskrit Literature ([Part 1](https://www.prekshaa.in/satirical-humour-modern-sanskrit-literature-%E2%80%93-1), [Part 2](https://www.prekshaa.in/satirical-humour-modern-sanskrit-literature-%E2%80%93-2))

  

The same publisher is shortlypublishing 5 new Sanskrit kāvyas by 3 authors. The [announcement](https://twitter.com/kashyapnnaik/status/1134814282447589376) mentions that the book release in Bengaluru will be accompanied by an “āśukavitā-goṣṭhi” where an assembly of 12 poets (including the 3 mentioned authors) will compose verses extempore on topics given by the audience.
