Without any doubt, there is no sandhi in परम्-आत्मा

However, I don't think we can exclude that this is a compound with an indeclinable at the first term.

I think this use is common, for example:

  

param-brahma = il trascendentale brahman  

param-parā = a series one after another

param-śabde =by the word param, or supreme  

param-tapa = destroying foes  

paraṃpadam =The abode of Viṣṇu  

paraṃparāka =Immolating an animal at a sacrifice  

paraṃparīṇa =Obtained by succession or descent, hereditary;  

paraṃparita =Continuous, forming a series.  

paraṃśakti =to the best of one's ability.  

paraṃkāryatva =the view that the subsequent संज्ञा or technical term should be preferred to the prior one, when both happen to apply simultaneously to a word.  

  

And why can't परम्-आत्मा (Transcendental spirit or soul) be right ?
