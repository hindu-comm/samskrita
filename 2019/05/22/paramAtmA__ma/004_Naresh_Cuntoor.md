Actually it doesn't. The question in Valerio's email had assumed paramaatma to be a compound word in the both the options. As Sri Bhat mentioned, the first option is not a compound word. So the question of two meanings of the compound does not arise.

  

  

  

> 
> > 
> > |     |                                                                                                                                                                | > |-----|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------| > |     | Param atma becoming paramatma is like dharman asheshena becoming dharmanasheshena in the preamble to the Vishnu sahasranama - just Sandhi without compounding, | >
> 
> > 
> > 
> > 

  

This is wrong.

If you take the two as independent words that are not in a compound, there is no sandhi in either of the two examples above.  

  

धर्मान् अशेषेण, परम् आत्मा - there is no sandhi going on here. If you think otherwise, please explain the relevant sutra.

  

Further, can you think of usages of paramaatmaa without the sense of a compound? I'd be curious to see such passages.
