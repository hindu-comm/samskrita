ज्ञेयं यत्तत्प्रवक्ष्यामियज्ज्ञात्वामृतमश्नुते ।  
अनादिमत्परंब्रह्मन सत्तन्नासदुच्यते ॥१३।१२॥   

  

Dear scholars

the meaning of this verse has been much debated among the ancient commentators. In particular the words अनादिमत्परंब्रह्मwhich can be separated in at least two ways:

  

अनादिमत्परंब्रह्मThe supreme brahman that is without beginning.

  

अनादि मत्परं ब्रह्म  Brahman without beginning,of which I alone am the supreme shelter (Sri Vishvanatha Chakravarthi Thakur)

  

having Me for the Highest(Sri Ramanuja,Sri Sridhara Swami,Sri Madhvacharya)

  

it is difficult to establish which is the right interpretation, but in the kūrma-purāṇa there is a similar phrase which presents no doubt.

  

अहं धाता जगद्योनिः स्वयंभूरेक ईश्वरः /

अनादिमत्परं ब्रह्म मामभ्यर्च्य विमुच्यते // २.३१.५//

  

Can it be used to establish the correctness of the second interpretation?  

  

thanks

valerio
