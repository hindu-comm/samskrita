No, the commentaries (except Padamanjari) are very explicit that this has to be पुत्र word. So your X must be पुत्र. And that is actually natural if you read the sutra and vartika in one flow by bringing the anvurvrutti of पुत्रस्य again in vartika - "नादिन्याक्रोशे पुत्रस्य, तत्परे च पुत्रस्य" - The "तत्परे च" merely extends the configuration from the sutra, saying "in presence of such a पुत्र word, this varika kicks in". नादिन्याक्रोशेे पुत्रस्य द्वित्वं न भवति (इति सूत्रम्), तत्परे (= एतादृशे पुत्रशब्दे परे) पुत्रस्य द्वित्वं न भवति (इति वार्त्तिकम्)।

Sorry, I do not know what is "Purva Pradhana Paramarshah". However,
even if "सः" represents पुत्रस्य, it cannot be any random पुत्र word, but only that which is followed by आदिनी (because vartika is given in the context of this sutra). So तत्परे will still mean तस्मिन् पुत्रशब्दे परे. Here तस्मिन् परे is sati-saptami.

  

BTW, in all the above discussion, we are assuming that तत्परे is सप्तमी. However, what नागेश says in this context is that तत्परे is actually षष्ठ्यर्थे सप्तमी, so it represents "तत्परस्य" (स्थानी) । Here, तत् actually represents "पुत्र" (of पुत्रादिनी) , and तत्पर represents another पुत्र that occurs before this पुत्र । अस्य पुत्रशब्दस्य स्थाने द्वित्वं न भवति - इति आशयः ।
