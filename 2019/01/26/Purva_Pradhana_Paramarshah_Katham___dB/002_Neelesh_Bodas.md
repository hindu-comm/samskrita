Please note - there are two "परे", one directly present in the vartika and other evident due to the usage of सप्तमी.

  

Just like अचि = "अच्" इत्यनेन निर्दिष्टे वर्णे परे

similarly तत्परे = "तत्पर" इत्यनेन निर्दिष्टे शब्दे परे

And what is तत्पर ? It refers to that पुत्र word which is followed by आदिनी । आदिनीपरः यः पुत्रशब्दः, सः "तत्परः" । तस्मिन् परे = आदिनीपरके पुत्रशब्दे परे । So that indicates the configuration where पुत्रादिनी is the second part of the word. Now, "पुत्रस्य" is still carried in the vartika, so that represents the first part of the word. Hence the resultant word becomes "आदिनीपरके पुत्रशब्दे परे पुत्रस्य द्वित्वम् न" । The word पुत्र does not get a द्वित्व when followed by - "*पुत्र that is followed by आदिनी*".

Thus we do not use "पुत्त्रपुत्रादिनि त्वमसि पापे", rather just "पुत्रपुत्रादिनि त्वमसि पापे । This is a the approach taken by बालमनोरमा and तत्त्वबोधिनी ।

  

पदमञ्जरी has a slightly different take on this. According to पदमञ्जरी, the word "तत्पर" represents any word (not necessarily पुत्र) which is followed by आदिनी. Thus, words like "पौत्रादिनी" etc also qualify for being "तत्पर". And when such a तत्पर is proceeded by the पुत्र word, then द्वित्वनिषेध happens. So, पुत्त्रपौत्रादिनि is wrong as per पदमञ्जरी, it should rather be "पुत्रपौत्रादिनि" (without द्वित्व).
