I have been misled by the conjugation of धा (4Ā) whose present participle seems to be dhayat.

# Conjugation tables of[*dhā_2*](https://sanskrit.inria.fr/DICO/34.html#dhaa%232)

### Present Active Participle dhayat[m.](https://sanskrit.inria.fr/cgi-bin/SKT/sktdeclin.cgi?q=dhayat;g=Mas;font=roma;r=dhaa%232;p=Ppra;lex=SH)[n.](https://sanskrit.inria.fr/cgi-bin/SKT/sktdeclin.cgi?q=dhayat;g=Neu;font=roma;r=dhaa%232;p=Ppra;lex=SH)dhayantī[f.](https://sanskrit.inria.fr/cgi-bin/SKT/sktdeclin.cgi?q=dhayantii;g=Fem;font=roma;r=dhaa%232;p=Ppra;lex=SH)

Is it possible that both meanings are correct?

Or is there anything that excludes the use of धा?
