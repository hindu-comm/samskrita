Ramana murthy,

  

Indicatory letters are a subset of Pratyayas.

  

Indicatory letters are the two great divisions of verbal roots, called conjugations.

i.e. The form the root takes before the addition of the personal endings, end in -a or does not.

All Class I, IV, VI and X verbs belong to the former.

All the other six classes have other stem forms.

Given that there are approx. 2,000 verb roots, you can easily scan though a descent online dictionary to obtain the

the information you need. Thus to tabulate that information according to your purpose -whatever that may be.

  

viz.

aṃś

   • cl. 10. P. aṃśayati.

  

Being a class 10 verb root, it tells you that it's indicatory letter is -a.
