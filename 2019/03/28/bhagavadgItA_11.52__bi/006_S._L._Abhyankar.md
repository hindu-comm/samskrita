नमस्ते वलेर्यो-वर्य !  

In most books शक्य अहम् is detailed as शक्यः अहम् But by rules of संधि शक्यः अहम्should become शक्योऽहम्

An optional thought is to consider शक्य अहम् to beशक्ये अहम् by the rule लोपः शाकल्यस्य (8'3'19)

शक्ये = शक् (4U)धातुः Here 4 A"To be able, to have power to effect."

Withशक्ये अहम् it becomesa curious construction in passive voice.

It is a good sentence to exercise with "change of voice".

Sentence we have in passive voice is अहं भक्त्या शक्ये

In active voice => भक्तिः मां शक्यते => by adding other words (अनन्या) भक्तिः मां (प्रवेष्टुं,द्रष्टुं तत्त्वेन ज्ञातुं च) शक्यति (or शक्यते or शक्नोति)

Note शक् is also 5 P. Henceशक्नोति

In Apte's dictionary, there is an interesting note about use ofशक् in passive voice => "*Pass.*To be able, be possible or practicable (giving a passive sense to a following infinitive); तत् कर्तुं शक्यते 'it can be done'."

  

Byभक्तिः मां शक्यते the capability to see Him gets assigned to Devotion and not to the Devotee. So, the capability to see Him also becomes impersonal ! Isn't that a charming interpretation ?

  

I know that many would not like this interpretation. But my interpretations are for self-satisfaction स्वान्तःसुखाय. So, I am not bothered, whether others like it or not.

  

Basically, I am more true to the grammar and such grammatical analysis reveals to me a new, though different perspective. I am happy !
