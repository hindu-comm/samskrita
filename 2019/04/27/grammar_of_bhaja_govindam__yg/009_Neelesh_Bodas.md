This question is a superb example of[कश्चित्कान्तान्याय](https://sa.wikiquote.org/wiki/%E0%A4%95%E0%A4%B6%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%9A%E0%A4%BF%E0%A4%A4%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%95%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%A8%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%A4%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%A8%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%AF%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%AF%E0%A4%83).

  

To answer your query, the story needs to be told. शङ्कराचार्य once saw an old man trying to loudly read and memorize the verbs डुकृञ् करणे and others from the Dhatupath. And then he said to the old man, "मूढमते, गोविन्दं भज, काले सम्प्राप्ते डुकृञ्करणे न हि रक्षति" - Pray to the god, idiot. When the death is approaching, the god will save you, not this डुकृञ्करणे.
