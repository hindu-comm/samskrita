Yes - if "dukrnj karane" is put in quotes it becomes grammatical, using an unusual word order.  

  

There is still a problem with "rakshati" - the sense of the verse is that grammar won't save you in some conditional future. It doesn't pertain to the present.

  

the future tense of rakshati is more apt here, analogous to  

  

हतो वा प्राप्स्यसि स्वर्गं जित्वा वा भोक्ष्यसे महीम् \|

  

from the Gita.
