"सुप्तिङ्ङन्तं पदम्" इति पाणिनीयं भाषितम्।"  

  

Though सुप्तिङ्ङन्तं पदम् is the commonly referred to definition of पद, there is a bit more to पाणिनीयपदम् than this. There are \[at least\] four Sutras dealing with the definition of पद in the अष्टाध्यायी.  
  
1.4.14 सुप्तिङ्ङन्तं पदम् (Definition #1)  
1.4.15 नः क्ये (अपवादसूत्र for Definition #1)  
1.4.16 सिति च (अपवादसूत्र for the अपवादसूत्र for Definition #2 - exception to an exception!)  
1.4.17 स्वादिष्वसर्वनामस्थाने (Definition #2)  
  
1.4.14 says any सुबन्त (one of the 21 inflected forms of a प्रातिपदिक) or तिङन्त (one of the \[2 x\] 9 x 10 conjugations of a धातु) is defined to be a पदम्.  
  
1.4.15 is an अपवादसूत्र for 1.4.14 which says that if one of the क्यच्, क्यष्, क्यङ् suffix follows something, then only a नकारान्त preceding form is defined as a पदम्. This prevents अनिष्ट forms like वाक्यति, तपर्यति, and समिद्यति by preventing the occurrence of चोः कुः, ससजुषो रुः, and झलां जशोऽन्ते, respectively, all of which have the पदान्त as a precondition. As a result the correct forms are वाच्यति, तपस्यति and समिध्यति. Note that the क्यच्, क्यष्, and क्यङ् suffixes arise from सुप आत्मनः क्यच् (3.1.8), लोहितादिडाज्भ्यः क्यष् (3.1.13) and कर्त्तुः क्यङ् सलोपश्च (3.1.11). Their being in third chapter is of relevance as seen in 1.4.17 below.  
  
1.4.16 says that when a सित् suffix like छस्, युस् or घस् follows something, then the preceding form is defined as a पदम्. This is the अपवादसूत्र for यचि भम् (1.4.18), which in turn is an अपवादसूत्र for definition #2 स्वादिष्वसर्वनामस्थाने (1.4.17). This results in forms like भवदीयः (via झलां जशोऽन्ते which operates at पदान्त in the case of भवत् + छस् -> भवत् + ईय् + अ), ऊर्णायुः (by preventing यस्येति च which operates on a भ in case of ऊर्णा + युस्) and ऋत्वियः (by preventing ओर्गुणः which operates on a भ in case of ऋतु + घस् -> ऋतु + इय् + अ). The suffixes छस्, युस् and घस् arise from भवतष्ठक् छसौ (4.2.114), ऊर्णाया युस् (5.2.123) and छन्दसि घस् (5.1.105). In these examples, a पद संज्ञा of the preceding form would have resulted from 1.4.17, but this would have been prevented by 1.4.18, so this सूत्र essentially prevents 1.4.18 from preempting 1.4.17.  
  
1.4.17 says that when a suffix from the fourth and fifth chapter, i.e. between 4.1.2 (स्वौजसमौट्छस्टाभ्याम्भिस्-ङेभ्याम्भ्यस्ङसिभ्याम्भ्यस्ङसोसाम्ङ्योस्सुप्) and 5.4.151 (उरःप्रभृतिभ्यः कप्), except for the सर्वनामस्थान suffixes (सु औ जस् अम् औट्), follows something then the preceding form is defined as a पदम्. This results in forms like षण्णाम्, वाग्भिः, राजभ्याम्, et cetera, instead of षष्णाम्, वाज्भिः and राजन्भ्याम्, due to झलां जशोऽन्ते which operates on a पदान्त (first two cases) and नलोपः प्रातिपदिकान्तस्य which also operates on a पदान्त. The नलोपः does not take place in case of राजन् + औ since औ is सर्वनामस्थान and hence in this case राजन् does not become a पद.
