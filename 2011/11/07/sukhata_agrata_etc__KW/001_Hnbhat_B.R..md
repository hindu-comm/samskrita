The first is not any suffix "da" according toPaninian Grammar and in this regard you are given to misunderstand. It is a primary derivative from the verb दा - दाने to give and hence it gets meaning.

  

The second belongs to the Secondary derivation and derived from any word by affixing the suffix तस् - तः which can be use in many senses derived by the case ending suffixes and hence called विभक्ति also.

It can be तृतीया, पञ्चमी, or सप्तमी as the case required.

  

सुखतः क्रियते रामाभोगः - here it is used in the sense either सुखेन क्रियते or सुखाय - सुखार्थं क्रियते; तृतीया or पञ्चमी.

  

in the example you cited, it is used in the sense सप्तमी, which can be explained as

  

अग्रे, पृष्ठे, पार्श्वे च -.

  

And in many cases, the words denoting distance or nearness or direction take पञ्चमी

  

दूरं ग्रामस्य, दूराद् ग्रामस्य, दूरेण् ग्रामस्य। अन्तिकं ग्रामस्य, अन्तिकाद् ग्रामस्य, अन्तिकेन ग्रामस्य।

  

in these cases, दूरतः also will convey the same meaning. It depends on the context.
