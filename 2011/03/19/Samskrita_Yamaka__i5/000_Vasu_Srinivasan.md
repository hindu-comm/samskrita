There are two slokas from "devotional" literature:  
  
One is very similar to the "na" Yamaka.  
  
For comparison -- from Kirataarjuniya:  
  
न नोननुन्नो नुन्नोनो नाना नानानना ननु ।  
नुन्नोऽनुन्नो ननुन्नेनो नानेन नुन्ननुन्ननुत् ॥  
  
From Sumadhva Vijaya Mahakaavya by Narayana Panditacharya:  
  
नाने नाने नेना नेनो नूने नन नुनुनना: \|  
नानाना नो नूनं नानेनानूननाsनुन्न: \|\| (Sarga 10, sloka 6)  
  
This particular sarga 10, every sloka is in a different meter, some of them are very fascinating.  
  
  
There is also a work by Sri Madhvacharya called Yamaka Bharata, which is short re-telling of Mahabharata replete with yamakas. One of the sloka is:  
  
भा भा भा भा भा भा भा भा  
भा भा भा भा भा भा भा भा \|  
भा भा भा भा भा भा भा भा  
भा भा भा भा भा भा भा भा \|\|  
  
Harsha's (or Bhoja?) sarasvati kantaabharaNam is full of similar slokas (probably its a compendium of slokas by various authors)
