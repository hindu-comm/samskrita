The verse quoted with the addition of the scribe Ganapati, into the 1st Chapter of Aadiparva, assigning a thousand number of KuTashloka-s:
which is not necessarily present in all the other editions, but in Geetha Press Edition: it is described as such clearly after the narration of the episode of Ganapati agreeing to be a scribe to Vyasa appears in Chapter 1. as the episode is narrated by SutapauraaNika:

  

ग्रन्थग्रन्थिं तथा चक्रे मुनिर्गूढं कुतूहलात्।

यस्मिन् प्रतिज्ञाय प्राह मुनिर्द्वैपायनस्त्विदम्॥८०॥

अष्टौ श्लोकसहस्राणि अष्टौ श्लोकशतानि च\|  

अहं वेद्मि शुको वेत्ति संजयो वेत्ति वा न वा\|\|॥८१॥

तच्छ्लोककूटमद्यापि सुदृढं ग्रथितं मुने।

भेत्तुं न शक्यतेऽर्थस्य गूढत्वाद् ग्रथितस्य च॥॥८२॥

etc.

  

before the अनुक्रमणिका of each of Parva-s in the 2nd Chapter.

  

Now it is open whether this above quoted verse is a कूट- or not.

  

Any how, I know that I do not know. According to some sources, it is the original count जयसंहिता by which Mahabharata was called in its initial stage. As the appeared with the episode of Ganesha becoming the scribe, it is accepted as the total number of कूटश्लोक-s in the whole text of Mahabharata. There is another count of total number of Samhita, too:

  

चतुर्विंशतिसाहस्रींचक्रे भारतसंहिताम।

उपाख्यानैर्विना तावद भारतं प्रोच्यते बुधैः ।

ततॊ ऽ ध्यर्धशतं भूयः संक्षेपं कृतवान ऋषिः ॥

अनुक्रमणिम अध्यायं वृत्तान्तानां सपर्वणाम॥ (आदिपर्व.अध्यायः १.६१-६२॥)
