Namaste,  
  
Two of the verses (with a minor change) below are also in the *Śuklayajurveda Mādhyandinasaṃhitā* and are read during *Sūryopasthāna* ritual of the daily *Sandhyā*. These are indeed beautiful verses and their recitation makes one feel the heat, the light and the energy of the *Sūrya*. I am providing the Hindi translation from the book *Sandhyopāsanā* (सन्ध्योपासना) by my Guru, if that helps you. The English translation is mine.

  
  
उदु त्यं जातवेदसं देवं वहन्ति केतवः ।  

दृशे विश्वाय सूर्यम् ॥७.४१॥

  
udu tyaṃ jātavedasaṃ devaṃ vahanti ketavaḥ ।  

dṛśe viśvāya sūryam ॥7.41॥  
संसार के दर्शन लिए सूर्य की किरणें समस्त अलौकिक जनों से सम्पन्न परम प्रकाशयुक्त उन सूर्य देवता को सुखपूर्वक नभोमण्डल में ले जा रही हैं।  
For the sight of the world, the rays of the *Sūrya* are bringing *Sūrya*, resplendent with the supreme light abounding with all the heavenly beings, to the sphere of sky.  
  
उद्वयं तमसस्परि स्वःपश्यन्त उत्तरम् ।  
देवं देवत्रा सूर्यमगन्म ज्योतिरुत्तमम् ॥ २०.२१ ॥  
udvayaṃ tamasaspari jyotiṣpaśyanta uttaram ।  
devaṃ devatrā sūryamaganma jyotiruttamam ॥20.21 ॥  
हम परमेश्वर का स्मरण करते हुए अन्धकार से दूर होकर स्वर्ग प्राप्त कर चुके हैं। हम देवलोक में भी देवताओं में श्रेष्ठ सूर्यदेव का दर्शन करते हुए उत्तम ब्रह्मज्योति का साक्षात्कार कर चुके हैं।  
Calling to mind the Supreme God, we are far removed from darkness and have achieved *Svarga.* Even in \[this\] world of the divine, coming face to face with *Sūrya*, the foremost among the *Deva*s, we have realized the light of the *Brahman*.  
  
Reference: Rambhadracharya, Swami (27 March 2007) (in Hindi). सन्ध्योपासना *\[The worship at Sandhy**ā\]*. Ghaziabad, Uttar Pradesh, India: Tulsi Mandal. pp. 13–14.  
  
Thanks, Nityanand
