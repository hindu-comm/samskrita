Far be it to restrict anyone's freedom in giving whatever they may  
like.

However, I would strongly advocate that the answers given be truthful,  
rather than fanciful.

I interpret the questions as being about languages "known well" -  
probably in order of fluency. Please, please write truthfully whatever  
those languages happen to be.

Censuses are conducted by governments not for the purpose of idealism  
and goals, but to know what the situation on the ground. Policies and  
policy statements are the correct place to write our hopes that do not  
happen to be currently extant.

It happens to be that I would like many people to study Sanskrit. But  
I would prefer to know for real how many people speak it fluently  
enough to be in their top 3.

I also prefer that people space their children and feed them well. But  
in the census, I entreat that people do not write in what they HOPE  
their children's ages are, or about their household food expenses  
would be in their IDEAL family budget. They should write what their  
actual situation happens to be.

I have heard alarmist emails and messages that "if we do not give  
Sanskrit as one of the choices, Sanskrit will be declared officially  
dead" !!! Is there an office of dead-language-declarations in India?

Regards,  
Dhananjay
