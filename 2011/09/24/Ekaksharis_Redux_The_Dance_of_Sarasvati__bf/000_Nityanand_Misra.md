[[Nityanand Misra	2011-09-24, 14:11:25 [Source](https://groups.google.com/g/samskrita/c/bfUFvZ3-8DM)]]



All  
  
I am pleased to inform you all of the publication of a Sanskrit Stotraprabandhakaavya (स्तोत्रप्रबन्धकाव्य) called Sitasudhanidhih (सीतासुधानिधिः) in praise of mother Sita. The work of 220 verses was composed this year in the month of May during the nine days of Vaishakha by the Pragyachakshu poet [Jagadguru Rambhadracharya](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jagadguru_Rambhadracharya) in Sitamarhi or Punyaranya - the manifestation place of mother Sita, during the Jagadguru's nine-day stay at Sitamarhi. The work was started on the first day of Vaishakha and completed on Sita Navami day (May 11, 2011).  
  
A Sanskrit copy of the work with a Sanskrit and Hindi commentary by the poet was published by the [Jagadguru Rambhadracharya Handicapped University](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jagadguru_Rambhadracharya_Handicapped_University) and the book was released on July 15, 2011.  
  
The पराऋतम्भरा प्रज्ञा of the poet has produced not one, not two, not three but eight एकाक्षरी श्लोकs (single consonant verses) in the third part work - from the antahstha and ushma consonants. The ekaksharis are given below for the rasikas and grammarians to have glimpse of this Vilasa of Sarasvati. I am also attaching a document which contains the Sanskrit only commentary on the Ekaksharis. The work also contains a triple-Mahayamaka - twelve occurrences of same quarter forming three verses which I will post shortly. Those interested in purchasing the book can get in touch with me offline.
