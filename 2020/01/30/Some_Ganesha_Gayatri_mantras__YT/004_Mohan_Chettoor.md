Quoting from Wikipedia:

"The***Ganapati Atharvashirsa***([Sanskrit](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit_language):गणपत्यथर्वशीर्ष,*Gaṇapatyarthavaśīrṣa*) is a Sanskrit text and a minor[Upanishad](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upanishad)of[Hinduism](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism). It is a late Upanishadic text that asserts that[Ganesha](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganesha)is same as the ultimate reality,[Brahman](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brahman).^([\[1\]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganapati_Atharvashirsa#cite_note-johngrimes21-1)[\[2\]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganapati_Atharvashirsa#cite_note-Courtright-2))The text is attached to the Atharvaveda,^([\[1\]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganapati_Atharvashirsa#cite_note-johngrimes21-1))and it is also referred to as the*Sri Ganapati Atharva Sirsha*, the*Ganapati Atharvashirsha*, the*Ganapati Atharvasirsa*, or the*Ganapati Upanishad*."
