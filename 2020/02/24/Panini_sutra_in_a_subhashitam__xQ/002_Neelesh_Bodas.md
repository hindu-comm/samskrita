"काचम्" , not कांच

  

A girl (who is creating a necklace), when asked - why are you putting a glass crystal, a precious bead, and an invaluable piece of gold in a single string ("sutra") replies - why can't I, if the greatthinker Panini can put a dog (श्वा), a young man (युवा) and the indra (मघवा) all in one sutra !  

  

Incidentally the sutra श्वयुवमघोनामतद्धिते talks about the process of सम्प्रसारणम्, which also has a common meaning of "To spread out / to extend" - which fits well in the current context of the puzzle (The three types of entities are "well spread out" in the necklace). यथा पाणिनिना एकस्मिन्नेव "सूत्रे" श्व-युव-मघोनाम् "सम्प्रसारणम्" उच्यते, तथैव बालया अपि एकस्मिन्नेव सूत्रे काच-मणि-काञ्चनस्य "सम्प्रसारणम्" क्रियते - इत्याशयः ।

  

As a complete digression, this is one of the very few sutras of Ashtadhyayi where the sutra is required to derive the word used in that very sutra ! ( श्वयुवमघोनामतद्धिते is used to derive मघोनाम् - The word that is the part of the sutra itself!).
