It should be दुर्धरधर्षिणि*दुर्मुखमर्षिणि.*

*  
*

Both acording to the commentary, is interpreted as the one who attacks the demons named Durdhara and Durmukha the commanders of Mahishasura enduring them or destroying them. Here the two are taken as the names of the commnders. Or durdhara or durmukha is be simply taken as adjective of demons as difficult to endure and talkative ones attccked by her. Both ways the two adjectives are commented.

  

In the translation, it is given as "molests demon Durdhara, destroys demon Durmukha" only.
