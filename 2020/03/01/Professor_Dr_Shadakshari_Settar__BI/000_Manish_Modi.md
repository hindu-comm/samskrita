Dr S. Settar was an internationally acclaimed multi-disciplinary scholar interested in art, archaeology, history, religion, philosophy and classical literature. He was born in Hampasagar (Ballari district) and died in Bengaluru. After graduating from Mysuru, he obtained PhDs from Karnataka University and Cambridge University.  
  
Career   
1960s — Professor of History and Archaeology, Karnataka University, Dharwad

  
1978 — Director, [National Museum Institute of the History of Art, Conservation and Museology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Museum_Institute_of_the_History_of_Art,_Conservation_and_Museology), New Delhi

  
1996 — President, [Indian Council of Historical Research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Council_of_Historical_Research), New Delhi

  

2005 — Honorary Director, Southern Centre of the [Indira Gandhi National Centre for the Arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indira_Gandhi_National_Centre_for_the_Arts), New Delhi

  

2002-2010 — Dr Sarvapalli Radhakrishnan Chair, National Institute for Advanced Studies, Bengaluru

  

2010 onwards — Professor Emeritus, National Institute for Advanced Studies, Bengaluru

+  
Visiting Professor at many prestigious universities like Harvard, Cambridge, Heidelberg, Athens, Leiden, Moscow, etc.
