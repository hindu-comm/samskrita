Dear Abhyankar, thanks for the beautiful annotation and elucidation of the word पुण्याहवाचन.

  

I think वाचन does not simply imply reading from a book, but reciting the word पुण्याह get done by the priests, as per the procedures laid down by the scriptures called प्रयोग with the rites accompanied as laid down in the प्रयोग books for each सूत्र and गोत्र. In Yajurveda itself, two प्रयोग-s are in practice बौधायनप्रयोग and आपस्तम्बीयप्रयोग. The question was about the sentence in the प्रयोग text in either of these and not about the meaning of the word पुण्याहवाचन.

  

uddhṛtya pīṭhake cāpi dvādaśāratnikocchrite / (20.5)

mahārājatavāsobhiḥ parikṣipya dhvajottamam / (20.6)

vāsobhir annapānaiśca pūjitair brāhmaṇarṣabhaiḥ / (20.7) 

puṇyāhavācanaṃ kṛtvā dhvaja ucchrīyate tadā / (20.8)

  

The above is the context of hoisting flag, called IndhradhvajamahaH in Mahabharata1, 57, 20.6-8

  

पुण्याहवाचनं दैवे ब्राह्मणस्य विधीयते।

एतदेव निरोंकारं कुर्यात् क्षत्रियवैश्ययोः॥

सोंकारं ब्राह्मणे ब्रूयात् निरोंकारं महीपतौ।

उपांशु च तथा वैश्ये शूद्रे स्वस्ति प्रयोजिते॥

  

इति विवाहावसरे यमस्मृतिः।

  

In other words, in the case of ब्राह्मण, ऒं पुण्याहम्,ऒं पुण्याहम्,ऒं पुण्याहम् pronouncing three times the word with ऒंकारः, and without ऒं in the case of Kshatriya and Vaishya, and in the case of with स्वस्ति
instead of it and in the case of वैश्य it is not announced muttering sound उपांशु only in the case of शूद्र.

  

This is explained in शब्दकल्पद्रुम -

  

पुण्याहस्य वाचनम् - दैवादिकर्मादौ मङ्गलार्थं पुण्याहमिति शब्दस्य वारत्रयकथनम्।

  

In other words, wishing Good Day for all the guests outside the context of स्मृति and प्रयोग text can be said as पुण्याहवाचन.

  

The usage of the prefixed verb नि\_नयति is found in different धर्मसूत्र texts only.

  

The other points in the sentence have already been explained by all the members in their replies.
