Dear Mr. Dipak Ashar,  
  
I wonder whether the responses of Dr. Ghare and Dr, Bhat left you wondering, where answer to your basic query of meaning of the word संनिभं is in their responses.  
May I venture a guess on this -

  
I think the word संनिभं has a derivation/composition from सं + नि + भ  
सं = संपूर्णतया = wholesomely  
नि = नितान्तम् = forever, eternally, without diminishing, up to the end  
भ = भाति इति = shines  

I also think that the two words काञ्चन संनिभं should actually be considered a single compound word काञ्चनसंनिभं and in turn as adjective of बृहस्पतिं. Hence both are in second case, singular.  
The compound word काञ्चनसंनिभ would mean काञ्चनस्य इव संपूर्णतया नितान्तं भाति इति = one who has a wholesome eternal splendour as of gold.  
It comes to mind that the meaning of comparison (as of) may also be implicit in सं, if सं = समम् (?)  
Hope, this helps.
