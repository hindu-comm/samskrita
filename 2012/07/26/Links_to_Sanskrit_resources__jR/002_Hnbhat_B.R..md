On Sat, Jul 28, 2012 at 6:50 PM, Subhash \<[subh...@gmail.com]()\> wrote:  

> Is there a way by which, any verb form can be looked up to find  
> a) the root (dhatu)  
> b) weather it goes by aatmane pada or parasmai pada ?

I do not understand you intention. If you get a form, you can analyze the root and gana with this link:

  

<http://tdil-dc.in/san/morph/index_dit.html>

  

to get the details you required as below for the form you mentioned.

  
आशास्महे  

|                                                              | |--------------------------------------------------------------| | आशास्महे=आङ्\_शास्1{कर्तरि;लट्;उ;बहु;आत्मनेपदी;शासुँ;अदादिः} |

|| || ||

  
Or the reverse, if you know the root, you can get the required form from this site:

  

<http://tdil-dc.in/san/skt_gen/verb/verb_gen.html>

  

but without prefixes. You have to add it.

  

आङ: शासु**इच्छायाम्**। आशास्ते। आयुराशास्ते। आशासाते।

  

If you know how to conjugate, you can use this site through the index, the root you want and navigate to the page:

  

<http://sanskrit1.ccv.brown.edu/Sanskrit/Vyakarana/Dhatupatha/mdhvindx/disp/mdhv_index.html>

  

you can find the relevant page for the verb you asked for

  

<http://sanskrit1.ccv.brown.edu/Sanskrit/Vyakarana/Dhatupatha/mdhvscan/disp/index.php?page=385>

  

with important forms given.
