Su - shobhanam, parva = amAsyAdi = festivals liks amAvAsyA (juncture of movement of Candra and Surya to another lunar digits - i.e. Full Moon etc. are called parva-s.) or in the alternative, the juncture of limbs aNgulyaadi the beautiful joints of fingers angulyaadi. This is the common style of commentaries.

The above commentary is depending on the religious literature. 'UrdhvamUlam adhaH shaakham ashvatthaM praahuravyayam etc. describes the ashvattha. And the ashvattha, is ---  
\* Urdhva-mUlam adhaZ-ZAkham aZvatthaM prAhur avyayam /  
\* chandAMsi yasya parNAni yas taM veda sa veda-vit //15.1//  
  
So many meanings can be derived from the vishtara depending on the context. shravas - is glory or fame.
