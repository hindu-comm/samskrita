In this case I think the application of 'namaH' has taken place as a  
free use of language which we come across in purAnic and other texts.Hence we  
shouldn't bring in the possibility of 'Nu stutau' here.It is clearly  
'namaH' in the given verse.

1\) If 'namaH' is used as an upapada then the use of accusative is  
wrong in 's'ivaM'.We can then go for 'bhramAt s'AbdabodhaH' and in our  
understanding substitute 's'ivaM' for 's'ivAya'.'Tat' implies here  
'taM' and then it would be substituted for 'tasmai'.  
2) Otherwise if 'namaH' is taken as a subanta then the sense can be  
construed in this manner : taM s'ivaM = tasmai s'ivAya esha  
namaH-s'abdaH prApnotu iti.

abhiyuktA agre pramANam iti.
