I have seen each response (and noted their tones, too!) Thank you :)

  

Thank you for pointing out about the 'numaH'. So, I do not have a question there, though I would go with the following anvaya :

यस्य आङ्गिकं भुवनं (अस्ति), यस्य वाचिकं सर्व-वाङ्मयम् (अस्ति) यस्य आहार्यं चन्द्र-तारादि (इति अस्ति), यस्य सात्त्विकं शिवम् अस्ति - तम्नुमः।

  

But, going back to the version as I had heard, and as seems to be popular ("A very common mistake made by most dancers."), I would like to ask if the following is acceptable.

यस्य आङ्गिकं भुवनं (अस्ति), यस्य वाचिकं सर्व-वाङ्मयम् (अस्ति) यस्य आहार्यं चन्द्र-तारादि (इति अस्ति), यस्य सात्त्विकं शिवम् अस्ति - तत् (तस्मै) नमः ।  

  

As आङ्गिकं,वाचिकं,आहार्यं,सात्त्विकं - are the four aspects ofअभिनय, I believe the four elements are listed and defined in the verse, as opposed to considering 'shivam' as a dvitIyA-vibhaktiH.

  

Now, nATyashAstra, verse 23, Chapter 6 says :

आङ्गिको वाचिकश्चैव ह्याहार्यस्सात्त्विकस्तथा ।

चत्वारोऽभिनया ह्येते विज्ञेया नाट्यसंश्रयाः ॥

  

So, more questions arise. Here in this shloka, the 'abhinaya's are mentioned in the puMlinga forms. In which case, the verse should be :

AngikaH bhuvanaM yasya vAchikaH sarva-vAngmayam(/H) ...

  

Again, thank you all in advance for responding to a sincere question.

A very common mistake made by most dancers.  
  
It is "tannumaH" = tam + numaH.  
numah - the verb is णु स्तुतौ 2nd conjugation नौति नुतः नुवन्ति, नौषि  
नुथः नुथ, नौमि नुवः नुमः  
So, tam sAtvikaM shivaM numaH
