Absence of svarAnkana does not mean the absence of svara-s. pANini clearly sets the svara to laukika as well as ChAndasa words.

  

>

> > All of the 100,000 verses of Mahabharata can be read, even when there > are no स्वराङ्कन-s.That does not diminish the importance of > स्वराङ्कन-s.They are important in specific contexts such as Vedic > texts. >
>

That's naturally the case as these verses are in the classical metres (predominantly anuShTubh) whose enunciation favors complete abandonment of svara-s in favor of the appropriate melody. This says nothing about non-metrical (and mostly non literary) language.
