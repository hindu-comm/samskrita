Dear Friends,

  

I am going through a thesis on Dentistry in Ancient Times. There the nomenclature of various teeth is given.

  

Kartanak - Incisors

Radanak - Canines

Chrvanak - Molars

  

The interesting part is that the English ( Latin) nomenclature is comparing the tooth to something outside. For example Canine is a tooth like that of dogs or Molar is a tooth that looks big whereas in Sanskrit it is as if a human being telling other human being about the way the tooth works. Charvanak is the one that grinds the food , Kartanak is the one that cuts the food and so on. Thus the Sanskrit nomenclature is much more consistent and scientific than the English one.

  

Please let me know your comments on this subject and examples from other areas. Thanks in advance.
